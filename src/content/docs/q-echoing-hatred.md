:::tip[Big win]
**Use a Trace of Echoes the moment you find one.** It does NOT stack, lives as a quest (not inventory item). Find one, drop everything, run it. Lose it if you don't.
:::

New Lord of Hatred content most players haven't done. Wave-survival mode, scales to your Pit ceiling. Decent reward density for the time invested, but a slow opening grinds because it always starts at wave 1.

## How to enter

1. Find a **Trace of Echoes** drop. Random world drop, looks like an amulet icon. Drops "anywhere in this color" (anywhere in the open world per author).
2. **Does NOT stack.** It's a quest, not inventory. Finding a second one before using the first deletes the first.
3. Triggers a wave-survival arena.

## Format

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| Mode | Wave survival (think Infernal Hordes but solo and scaling) |
| Difficulty scaling | Every 10 waves = +1 Torment tier (e.g. wave 80 = Torment 8) |
| Reward density | Decent, scales with how high you push |
| Run time | 10-15 minutes per attempt |
| Skip mechanic | None. Even if you one-shot the screen, you still wait for the next wave |

## Why people don't do them

The first ~80 waves are tedium if your character can already clear Torment 12. Author note: "I literally one shot everything for the first 80 waves." There's no way to start at a higher wave even when you're geared. Likely a future patch buff target. Until then, treat it as a 10-15 minute commitment per Trace, not a quick activity.

## When to skip

If you're early Torment (1-5) and don't have a power spike on the horizon, you can hold a Trace briefly. Don't hold long — too easy to find a second and waste the first. Default: use it when you find it.

## What you're trading

Time per run is the cost. Reward is decent loot density plus the rare/late-wave mythic chance. Not the best XPH or seals-per-hour in the game (see [§I. Mythic Seal Loop](/i-mythic-seal-loop/) for those), but a nice palate cleanser and a guaranteed reward floor.
