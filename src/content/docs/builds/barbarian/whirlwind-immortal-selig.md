Whirlwind Barbarian variant that becomes effectively immortal by running zero Maximum Life and channeling all incoming damage through resource via Melted Heart of Selig. Slower kill speed than the d4builds variant 5 but you basically don't die.

## Damage profile

Damage is **Overpower-driven**, not raw Fury-spend. Overpower fires guaranteed crits scaled to Fortify total, which is amplified by every Fortify-boosting affix and aspect. The build's identity isn't "spin fast" — it's "spin while immortal long enough to win every fight."

Three contributing buckets:

- **Overpower bucket** — Overpower Damage Multiplier affixes on rings + amulet, plus Overheating Aspect Overpower chance on slain Burning enemies
- **Fortify bucket** — Crushing Aspect (while Fortified) + Iron Skin uptime + Fortify generation affixes
- **Channel + Berserk bucket** — same Aspect of Channeling + Ugly Bastard / Heir of Perdition as the meta variant

## Key gear (priority order)

| # | Slot | Item | Why mandatory |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Amulet** | **Melted Heart of Selig** (mythic) | THE build-defining item. Drains your maximum Life pool, channels incoming damage to primary resource. While you have Fury you cannot die. |
| 2 | **Helm** | Heir of Perdition (mythic) OR Ugly Bastard Helm | Same as the meta variant |
| 3 | **Gloves** | Gohr's Devastating Grips | Same — 75–90%x WW damage + pull |
| 4 | **Chest** | Aspect of Disobedience | DoT-stacking damage reduction. Critical in the no-life build because Selig consumes Fury per damage taken — you want to take less. |
| 5 | **Pants** | Aspect of Anger Management | +Berserking and damage above 50 Fury |
| 6 | **Boots** | Overheating Aspect | Overpower chance + fire damage |
| 7 | **Ring 1** | Crushing Aspect | 45–65%x while Fortified. Iron Skin uptime keeps this on. |
| 8 | **Ring 2** | Aspect of Berserk Fury | Resource sustain so Selig doesn't drain to zero |
| 9 | **2H Bludgeoning** | Aspect of Channeling | Same |
| 10 | **2H Slashing** | Cremator's Aspect | Same |
| 11 | **DW1** | Vehement Brawler's Aspect | Same |
| 12 | **DW2** | Ramaladni's Magnum Opus | Same |

**Gems:** Grand Ruby in weapons + armor, Grand Diamond in jewelry. With Selig you can swap to Grand Skulls in jewelry for armor if you're getting overwhelmed by physical hits before Selig converts them.

## Skills & rotation

Same skill bar as the meta variant: Whirlwind · Iron Skin · Wrath of the Berserker · Rallying Cry · War Cry · Challenging Shout.

**Rotation difference vs meta:** You weight Iron Skin and Challenging Shout HIGHER because Fortify and damage reduction directly extend Selig's drain runway. You can be more aggressive about staying inside enemy packs because Selig channels the damage through resource instead of life.

## Paragon, glyphs, talisman

Same Sescheron's Fury 5-set + Banished Lord's Talisman charm setup. The 5-set's +50 Max Fury is even more valuable in the immortal variant because Fury is your effective HP bar.

## What to avoid

:::danger[Don't]
- Don't stack Maximum Life affixes — they're wasted slots in the no-life build. Use those slots for Fury Generation, damage multipliers, or resistances instead.
- Don't drop below ~100 Fury sustained. That's the Sescheron's Fury Colossal threshold AND your Selig damage runway. Both vanish together if you go below.
- Don't use Selig before Patch 3.0.2 buffs landed if you're reading this guide later — the build assumes current patch behavior.
- Don't ignore resistances. Selig converts damage to resource at a 1:1-ish ratio; reducing the damage taken in the first place stretches Fury further.
:::

:::caution[Caveat]
- Single-target damage is lower than the meta variant because you're not pushing every offensive multiplier. The trade is survivability.
- Some ground effects bypass Selig entirely (specifically certain DoT pools). Watch for those — Selig isn't actually immortal vs every damage type.
- The build feels weaker until you get Melted Heart of Selig. Until then you're playing a strict downgrade of the meta Whirlwind.
:::
